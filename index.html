About

Chris is a Computing Science graduate from Hull, Teesside, and Abderdeen Universities, with a strong and focused love of coding in C/C++, while experimenting with technology and hardware. When he's not trying to join together software or hardware into something new and potentially useful, you can often find him coding, soldering, dismantling and rebuilding electronics, and reading about what's going on in the world of tinkerers, hackers, developers and gaming. Projects range from small to medium apps (usually games, hardware sensor or graphics-based), upgrading or customizing joypads and fixing up old consoles and laptops to having some downtime with a really good book (although it normally is a CS or games development-related book to be fair).

But there's more to life (although he sometimes won't admit it) - when he's not describing himself in third-person on his portfolio, he may be watching oddball world cinema, nerdy TV shows, animated sitcoms or Blade Runner, you might find him out and about with friends (at the cinema, music festivals, or even just out for a meal), or on XBOX Live/Steam (talking about oddball world cinema and Blade Runner). Sometimes, he even plays the guitar really badly.

Chris has worked over the years as an opthalmic assistant, digital imaging assistant in photography equipment sales, a loss prevention officer, in outdoor equipment sales and repairs (camping) and electronics repairs (laptop, PC and game console hardware and software repairs), has great communication skills, and is very used to working in small to large teams, and even alone, where every job is a challenge and opportunity to learn new skills and techniques to succeed in both work and life

Chris has also had a very keen interest in computing and gaming since he was 4, when the family acquired an MSX1 home computer, and moved through an Atari ST and numerous IBM compatible Windows/Linux PCs, all of which he has programmed small apps and games. He also owned a NES, Gameboy, SNES, Dreamcast, PSOne, XBOX, XBOX 360 and XBOX One - most of these he has repaired, upgraded and modded, and a few of these he has tried some development with their SDKs and homebrew, and would love the opportunity to work with console hardware in the future.
